Publications

Baricitinib treatment resolves lower-airway macrophage inflammation and neutrophil recruitment in SARS-CoV-2-infected rhesus macaques

SARS-CoV-2 infection models show that Baricitinib treatment reduces inflammation and neutrophil recruitment in the lungs. The study supports its potential as a frontline treatment for COVID-19-related inflammation.
A modified vaccinia Ankara vector-based vaccine protects macaques from SARS-CoV-2 infection, immune pathology, and dysfunction in the lungs

This study demonstrates that an MVA-based COVID-19 vaccine induces strong immune responses and provides protection against SARS-CoV-2 infection and related lung pathology in macaques.
Shared transcriptional profiles of atypical B cells suggest common drivers of expansion and function in malaria, HIV, and autoimmunity

Identifies common transcriptional profiles in atypical B cells across malaria, HIV, and autoimmune diseases, revealing shared mechanisms driving their expansion and function.
Correlation Between TIGIT Expression on CD8+ T Cells and Higher Cytotoxic Capacity

Explores how TIGIT expression on CD8+ T cells correlates with cytotoxic capacity in HIV-infected and healthy individuals, suggesting TIGIT+ cells maintain intrinsic cytotoxicity despite exhaustion markers.
Tau-typing: a Nextflow pipeline for finding the best phylogenetic markers in the genome for molecular typing of microbial species

Introduces Tau-typing, a Nextflow pipeline for identifying genomic markers with high phylogenetic resolution, particularly useful for organisms with limited culture-based methods.
